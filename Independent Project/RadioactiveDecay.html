Radioactive Decay
What is radioactive decay? Well remember the isotope 'Carbon 14'? That was an isotope that undergoes radioactive decay, but not just isotopes undergo this process, atoms, elements, isotopes, etc. do this as well. You have probably heard that if a nuclear weapon goes of that you need to wait 35 years before coming back to the surface, because the bombs radiation has a 'half-life' of 35 years, meaning the unstable part of the atom is gone after that time. Carbon 14, has a half-life of 5,730 years! Anyway Radioactivity is the release of energy from the decay of the nuclei. Quoting from The DOE "Radioactive isotopes are also critical to modern society, with uses in medicine, chemistry, energy, environmental sciences, material sciences, manufacturing, and national security." this goes to show how much radioactive materials are used in our society.
