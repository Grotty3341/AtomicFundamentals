Atomic Models
First off, what are atomic models? How do they work? What do they do? Well, atomic models are used to define the atom's structure, they are theoretical "models" based experimental evidence. This started a long time ago when John Dalton proposed his atomic theory sometime around 1808, this was the first rough model and one of the only reconized model for a while until 1904 when the Plum Pudding Model was proposed by J. J. Thompson.
