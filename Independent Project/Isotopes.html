Isotopes
Isotopes, you've probably heard of the term but not know exactly what it was, but its actually pretty simple. Isotopes are parts of an element that have the same number of protons but adifferent amount of neutrons. The number of protons in a nucleus determines the elementâ€™s atomic number on the Periodic Table. Carbon, has 6 protons meaning it's atomic number is 6. Carbon also has 3 natural isotopes: carbon 12, carbon 13, and carbon 14, each having 6 protons and 6 neutrons, adding 1 neutron as the number gets larger. Adding neutrons though makes the isotope's proporties changes immensely. This change can make isotopes unstable and radioactive, meaning they will slowly decay.

(Types of Carbon Isotopes)
Unstable Isotopes, are mainly caused when a neutron is added to the nucleus, but sometimes it takes 2 or more to make it unstable. For example Carbon 12 is a stable isotope as well as Carbon 13, but adding one more neutron makes it unstable, Carbon 14 is classifed as an Unstable isotope. Unstable isotopes are very helpful in medicine, industry, and even power. Iodine isotopes are used in medicine, to find brain tumors, where as uranium or plutonium isotopes are used for nuclear power generation.
